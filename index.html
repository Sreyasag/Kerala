Renowned for its scenic locations and natural beauty, Kerala is sure to enchant any nature lover with its hills, backwaters, beaches, waterfalls and wildlife. The state has a rich heritage and thriving culture one can explore at various forts, palaces, museums, monuments and pilgrim centers.

Districts

Alappuzha

The district is best known for its picturesque backwaters, by which it is well connected to other parts of Kerala, including Kumarakom, the district being a well known tourist destination. It is also known for its coir factories.

Ernakulam

Situated almost at the middle of Kerala State and on the coast of the Arabian Sea. Ernakulam District is Commercial capital of Kerala as it is the major business hub and the highest revenue yielding district in the state.

Idukki

Idukki means a place with a deep gorge. River Periyar flowed through a canyon between two rocky mountains. This beautiful High range district of Kerala is geographically known for its mountainous hills and dense forests.

Kannur

To the east the district is bounded by the Western Ghats. The Arabian Sea lies to the west.Kannur District is known as the land of looms and lores. The district is a major centre of Theyyam, a ritual dance of northern Kerala.

Kasaragod

Kasargod is the northernmost district of Kerala. The district is known for its coir and hand loom industries.The Ghats dominate the topography. The district is popular with the forts, rivers, hillocks, beaches and shrines.

Kollam

About 30 percent of the district is covered by Ashtamudi Lake making it a gateway to the Kerala backwaters. Plains, mountains, mountains, lakes, forests, farmland and rivers make up the topography of the district.
